# T3.4 · Experience Sampling with Data Foundry

**Location:** `textbook/3/collection.tex:194–197`
**Textbook context:** ESM; prompting strategy, frequency, combining sensors with self-report.
**Data Foundry features:** Experience Sampling Dataset (PIEL integration), Survey/Form Dataset, optional Fitbit/wearable dataset
**Platform pages:** [Experience Sampling guide](https://data-foundry.net/Guides/Examples/Integrations/experiencesampling.html) · [Survey Dataset](https://data-foundry.net/Learning/Datasets/DatasetsOverview/surveydataset.html)

## Steps

1. **Design the probe** – Write 3–5 survey items (mood, context, brief activity label). Choose a prompting strategy: random, fixed-schedule, or event-triggered.
2. **Setup in Data Foundry** – Create an Experience Sampling Dataset; upload your PIEL survey file; register participants.
3. **Optionally link a wearable** – Add a Fitbit or Google Fit dataset for the same participants to capture passive physiological context alongside self-reports.
4. **Run a pilot** – Collect 2–3 days of data from yourself or a partner. Check completion rates and prompt timing.
5. **Export & merge** – Use the Data Foundry export tool to combine ESM responses with wearable data; open in a Starboard Notebook and plot responses over time.
6. **Reflect** – When did participants skip prompts? What does that tell you about burden? How does the passive data confirm or contradict self-reports?
