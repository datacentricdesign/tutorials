# T4.1 · Exploring Time-Series Data

**Location:** `textbook/4/exploration.tex:278–281`
**Textbook context:** Time-series techniques: decomposition, smoothing, anomaly detection, aggregation.
**Data Foundry features:** IoT Dataset, Starboard Notebook
**Platform pages:** [Starboard Notebook](https://data-foundry.net/Guides/Tools/Starboard/StarboardNotebook.html) · [Data Visualisation Tutorial](https://data-foundry.net/Tutorials/DataVisualisationTutorial.html)

## Steps

1. **Load data** – Open a Starboard Notebook linked to an IoT Dataset collected in T3.3 (or a provided sample dataset). Print the first rows; check timestamp format and sampling regularity.
2. **Plot raw signal** – Line plot the full time series. Identify obvious patterns and anomalies visually.
3. **Smooth** – Apply a rolling mean (e.g. 5-point window). Overlay on the raw signal. Adjust the window and observe how detail is lost.
4. **Aggregate** – Resample to hourly and daily totals. Produce a heatmap (hour of day × day of week). What rhythms emerge?
5. **Detect anomalies** – Flag values beyond 2 standard deviations from the rolling mean. What do they correspond to in context?
6. **Prepare for PDA** – Export a clean visualisation (e.g. the heatmap) suitable for showing to a participant. What question will you ask them about it?
